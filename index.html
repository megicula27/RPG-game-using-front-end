Magic: The Gathering's lore

Choose your Hero!

Urza

Urza is a cunning, scheming, but just jerk who might very well be a villain with good intentions. However, he did destroy Phyrexia and Yawgmoth, which solidifies his anti-hero status

Tefari

Teferi is one of the few characters from the earlier years of Magic to still be relevant within the game's lore. As a time mage, Teferi is a notable and powerful theme that can cohesively tie a series of spells together.

Karn

While many players primarily associated Karn with the strength of the card, Karn Liberated, he is one of the few characters that bridge the earlier years of Magic lore with the current era. First appearing all the way back in Urza's Saga in 1998 as Karn, Silver Golem, Karn has served prominent positions in both the Scars of Mirrodin Block as well as Dominaria.

Sorin Markov

Few Planeswalkers are as closely tied to their home plane as the vampiric Sorin Markov is to the plane of Innistrad. As the immortal creator of the iconic Avacyn, Sorin is both inegrally connected to Innistrad's history as well as the history of the Eldrazi.

Nissa Revane

Perhaps one of the safest guesses of a character to receive a Signature Spellbook, Nissa Revane is a prominent core figure in Magic's lore and is one of the characters most closesly affiliated with green mana.
